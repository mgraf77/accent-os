# INFORMATION DENSITY FRAMEWORK — ACCENTOS
> **Objective:** Cognitive Speed over Visual Polish

AccentOS UX prioritizes the speed of decision over the beauty of the interface. We use "Functional Density" to maximize the amount of actionable intelligence visible on a single screen.

---

## 1. THE TYPOGRAPHY DUALITY
We use two fonts to separate "Language" from "Data."

- **OUTFIT (Sans-Serif):** The UI Framework. Used for labels, navigation, explanations, and titles. It provides a modern, approachable shell.
- **DM MONO (Monospaced):** The Business Intelligence. Used for SKUs, prices, percentages, dates, and performance metrics. Monospacing ensures that columns of numbers align perfectly, allowing for faster ocular scanning.

---

## 2. PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE RULES
Density must not lead to confusion. We follow the "Drill-Down" rule.

- **Level 1 (Surface):** The status dot or sparkline. Tells you IF there is an issue.
- **Level 2 (Card):** The Signal summary. Tells you WHAT the issue is.
- **Level 3 (Panel):** The detail view. Provides the DATA needed to fix it.
- **Level 4 (Transactional):** The Workflow. The specific TOOL needed to resolve the issue.

---

## 3. COLOR AS INTELLIGENCE
Color is never decorative in AccentOS.

- **Red (#ed1c24):** Requires immediate action. Failure, Critical Risk, or Loss.
- **Yellow/Amber:** Warning. Deterioration detected. Action needed within 24-48h.
- **Green:** Healthy / Target Met.
- **Blue:** Informational / Active. Used for buttons and active states.
- **Gray:** Passive / Historical. Used for background data and "Inactive" states.

---

## 4. THE 3-SECOND SCAN RULE
Every "Operational Surface" must be designed so that the most important fact is identifiable within 3 seconds of load.

- **Contrast:** Use high-contrast backgrounds for critical metrics.
- **Positioning:** Top-left is for the primary signal.
- **Aggregation:** Groups of items should be summarized by a "Status Stripe" at the top of the container.

---

## 5. DENSITY BENCHMARKS
- **Desktop:** Target 30-50 actionable "objects" per view (e.g., 50 rows in a table, or 12 high-density cards).
- **Mobile:** Target 5-8 actionable "objects" per view.
- **Whitespace:** Minimal. We prioritize fitting the data over "breathing room." Information density = operational velocity.
