# ATTENTION COMPRESSION RULES — Protecting the Executive Triage Loop

## 1. THE ATTENTION TAX LAW
Attention is the business's most expensive non-renewable resource. Every pixel, word, and digit on a command surface incurs an **Attention Tax**.
*   **Rule:** If the cognitive cost of processing a piece of data exceeds the potential ROI of acting on it, that data must be compressed or hidden.

## 2. THE THREE-SECOND REJECTION
If a signal cannot be understood and triaged within 3 seconds, it is too noisy.
*   **Rule:** Any signal failing the 3-second triage must be sent back to the intelligence engine for further compression or aggregation.

## 3. ZERO-SUM VISIBILITY
The executive surface has a finite attention budget.
*   **Rule:** Adding a new signal or metric requires removing or aggregating an existing one. Dashboard creep is treated as operational debt.

## 4. AGGREGATION BY DEFAULT
Individual anomalies are for operators; patterns are for executives.
*   **Rule:** Single-instance alerts (e.g., "One quote stale") must be held in the operator queue and only promoted to the executive surface once they form a cluster (e.g., "5 quotes stale in Visual Comfort").

## 5. THE "SO WHAT?" FILTER
Data without a clear "Next Action" is just noise.
*   **Rule:** No metric or signal may be displayed without an associated action button (Resolve, Snooze, or Draft) or a clear directional momentum indicator.

## 6. TRUST OVER TRANSPARENCY
Providing too much raw data to "show the work" increases cognitive load and degrades trust.
*   **Rule:** Hide the math. Show the result. Only reveal raw data on a "Deep Dive" long-press.

## 7. MOMENTUM OVER MAGNITUDE
The direction of travel is often more important than the current value.
*   **Rule:** Prioritize directional indicators (↑/↓) and acceleration markers over large static numbers.
