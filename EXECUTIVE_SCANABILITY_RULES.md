# EXECUTIVE_SCANABILITY_RULES.md

These rules ensure that the executive operations layer remains low-noise and highly efficient for busy operators on mobile devices.

## 1. Information Density Rules
- **Rule of One:** Each card or tile should communicate exactly **one** primary fact or trend.
- **The "So What?" Filter:** No data point should be shown without a comparison (Target, Prior Month, or Industry Benchmark).

## 2. Hierarchy Rules
- **Severity First:** Critical signals always appear at the physical top of the feed.
- **Action over Data:** Buttons (☏ Call, ✉ Email, ✓ Resolve) should be as prominent as the numbers.

## 3. Visual Escalation
- Use **color sparingly.** If everything is red, nothing is red.
- **Dotted Underlines** for low-confidence data.
- **Pulse Animations** (limited to Level 5) for systemic failures.

## 4. Scan-First Ergonomics (Mobile)
- **Thumb-Zone Actions:** All navigation and critical action buttons must be in the bottom 50% of the mobile screen.
- **Minimum Font Sizes:** Labels (11px Bold), Primary Numbers (22px Mono), Body text (14px Outfit).

## 5. Cognitive Load Reduction
- **No Spreadsheets:** Executives should never see a table with more than 5 columns on a mobile device.
- **Rounded Numbers:** $1.2M is better than $1,245,678.92.
- **Relative Timestamps:** "2 hours ago" or "Yesterday" instead of "2026-05-04 14:22:01".

## 6. Readability Rules
- **Contrast:** Ensure all status badges meet WCAG AA contrast standards (especially white-on-yellow).
- **Whitespace:** 16px minimum padding between executive pulse cards to prevent "visual clutter".
