# EXECUTIVE NOISE REDUCTION PATTERNS — Design for Scan Velocity

## 1. THE "SIX DOT" SUMMARY (DIMENSION COMPRESSION)
Instead of 50 individual metrics, we compress the entire business health into six dots representing Inventory, Vendor, Fulfillment, Ecommerce, Operational, and Data Risk.
*   **Pattern:** If a dot is green, the executive ignores the entire dimension. If amber/red, the executive taps to expand *only* the anomalies within that dimension.

## 2. THE "RULE OF ONE" CARD
Avoid cluttered cards with 5-10 data points.
*   **Pattern:** One primary metric in DM Mono (large). One primary reason (text). One primary action (button).

## 3. SEMANTIC COLOR COMPRESSION
Color should only be used to signal **Urgency**, not for decorative categorization.
*   **Pattern:** Gray is the default state for the business. Red/Orange/Yellow are only for "Attention Required." Green is only for "Action Successful."

## 4. TEMPORAL COMPRESSION (SNOOZE & SUPPRESS)
Noise often comes from signals that aren't ready to be acted upon.
*   **Pattern:** "Remind me when this changes by >10%" or "Snooze until Monday morning." This removes the signal from the attention budget without deleting it.

## 5. AGGREGATE "SITUATIONAL" CARDS
Instead of 10 cards for 10 stale quotes, show one card.
*   **Pattern:** "12 Stale Quotes ($45k Total). Top risk: Project X."
*   **Triage:** Tapping "Resolve" on the aggregate card opens a sub-queue for rapid-fire "Next, Next, Next" triage.

## 6. CONFIDENCE-BASED DIMMING
Uncertainty creates cognitive friction.
*   **Pattern:** If a signal is based on stale data, desaturate the card (make it gray/faded). This signals "Awareness only; do not act yet."
