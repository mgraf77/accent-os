# MOBILE ATTENTION BUDGETS — Cognition on the Move

## 1. THE 30-SECOND CHECK-IN BUDGET
The mobile surface is designed for the "Gap between Tasks."
*   **Budget:** The executive should be able to clear their high-severity queue in < 30 seconds.
*   **Constraint:** If the total time to read and triage the Tier I/II queue exceeds 30 seconds, the system is failing its compression duty.

## 2. THE THUMB-ZONE ECONOMY
Interaction cost is a component of the attention budget.
*   **Budget:** All primary "Triage" actions must be within the bottom 40% of the screen.
*   **Constraint:** No navigation or triage action should require a second hand or a precision tap.

## 3. GLANCEABLE DATA LIMITS
Cognitive load increases exponentially with each additional number.
*   **Budget:** Max **3 large numbers** visible on the screen at any one time.
*   **Constraint:** Any secondary data must be hidden behind a "Peek" (long-press) or "Expand" interaction.

## 4. THE "UNREAD" CEILING
A long list of unread signals creates "Productivity Debt" and leads to avoidance.
*   **Budget:** The Tier I/II queue should never exceed **7 active items**.
*   **Constraint:** If the queue hits 8 items, the bottom 2 must be auto-compressed into a "More Anomalies" summary card.

## 5. NOTIFICATION HYGIENE
Haptic and sound alerts are the highest-cost attention taxes.
*   **Budget:** **Zero** notifications for Tier III/IV/V.
*   **Constraint:** Haptic pings are reserved strictly for Tier I (Critical) and Tier II (High-Value) signals.
