# SIGNAL OPERATOR REALITY AUDIT
> **Version:** 1.0 | **Status:** DRAFT | **Perspective:** Operator Experience (OX)

## 1. COGNITIVE OVERLOAD RISK
**Current State:**
- The system surfaces a "Daily Brief" with multiple tiles (Tiles for Unverified Scores, Tier C, Activity, Unassigned Reps, etc.).
- There are separate "Alerts" and "Decision Engine" tabs.
- Information is dense and requires context-switching between tabs.

**Risk Assessment:**
- **HIGH:** Operators are presented with a "wall of tasks" upon login. Without clear primary focus, the "Rule of One" is violated.
- **DANGER:** The distinction between an "Alert" (stale data/risk) and a "Recommendation" (sales opportunity) is blurred in the UI.

## 2. QUEUE USABILITY
**Current State:**
- Alerts are in a flat list with basic filtering (Status, Severity).
- Decision Engine is a table with impact-based sorting.
- Navigation is "Jump to Page" via buttons.

**OX Friction:**
- **Tab Fatigue:** Moving from a "Quote Stale" alert to the Quote Generator and then back to the Alerts list is a high-friction loop.
- **Context Loss:** The operator loses the "Why" once they navigate away from the signal source.

## 3. ESCALATION ERGONOMICS
**Current State:**
- Escalation is implicit through severity colors (Urgent/Red, Warn/Yellow, Info/Blue).
- No formal "escalation path" for unresolved signals.

**OX Friction:**
- **Silence as Success:** Signals that aren't actioned simply stay in the list. There is no "vibration" or "heat" generated by a signal that persists across multiple days.

## 4. EXECUTIVE SCAN USABILITY
**Current State:**
- Dashboard provides a high-level summary but lacks a "Pulse" indicator.
- Stats are raw numbers ($ Revenue, Count of Deals) rather than directional momentum.

**OX Friction:**
- **The "So What?" Gap:** An executive sees "14 Stale Deals" but doesn't know if that is *improving* or *deteriorating* compared to yesterday.

## 5. ALERT FATIGUE RISK
**Current State:**
- Heuristics run on every hydration.
- Alerts can be dismissed, but dismissed alerts re-emerge if the condition persists.

**Risk Assessment:**
- **CRITICAL:** If an operator cannot "fix" the underlying data (e.g., a PO is overdue but the vendor is slow), the alert becomes noise. Dismissal is currently a temporary suppression, leading to "Snooze Loop" fatigue.

## 6. MOBILE OPERATOR REALITY
**Current State:**
- Responsive design collapses columns into a single stack.
- Buttons are appropriately sized for touch.

**OX Friction:**
- **Scrolling Depth:** On mobile, the "Daily Brief" can take up 3-4 screen heights before reaching actual work surfaces.
- **Action Density:** "Open ->" buttons are small and require precision that isn't always available in a warehouse or showroom setting.

### SUMMARY OF OX DEBT
| Category | Friction Score (1-10) | Primary Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Cognitive Load | 7 | Severity-based grouping (Escalation Hierarchy) |
| Queue Flow | 8 | "In-place" actions (Action Slugs) |
| Executive Scan | 6 | Directional Momentum Indicators |
