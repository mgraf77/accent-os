# ADR-005: 14-category vendor scoring rubric

**Status**: Locked  
**Date**: 2026-05 (v6.9.4 scoring engine)

## Decision

Score all vendors on 14 weighted categories (0–10 scale each). Weights sum to 100%. Tier assignment via adaptive percentile distribution over live scores.

## Context

Accent needed an objective, defensible framework for comparing 240+ vendors across financial terms, marketing support, and market position. Subjective "gut feel" rankings were inconsistent and uncommunicable.

## Categories and weights

Financial Terms: Rebates(10), Discounts(8), Credit Terms(6), Freight(8), Returns/RGA(7), IMAP/Markup(8)  
Sales/Marketing: Marketing Funds(7), Display(6), Lights America(5), Web Listing(4), Rep Score(8), DTC(7), L1 Member(6), Demand(10)

## Rationale

- 14 categories cover every commercial dimension that affects Accent's economics
- Numeric rubric (RUBRIC_NUMERIC) for objective categories: rebates%, discount%, credit days, freight threshold, IMAP multiplier, search volume
- Composite scoring (RUBRIC_COMPONENTS) for multi-factor categories: marketing funds, display program
- Binary scoring for L1 member, Lights America (binary gates, not spectrums)
- Adaptive tier percentiles: tiers recalculate as new vendors are scored, avoiding fixed boundaries that age poorly
- Weight scenarios support: current weights are default; Michael can test alternative weightings in the UI

## Consequences

- 226 lower-volume vendors bulk-scored at 3 (deferred — see v6.9.3)
- Top 30 by sales scored individually via web research
- Score states: `scored | unverified | needs_review | n/a` tracked per category per vendor
- Rubric pages: [[rubric-rebates]] through [[rubric-demand]] document the full scale

## Alternatives considered

- Simple 5-point scale: rejected (loses nuance for numeric categories)
- 20+ categories: rejected (diminishing returns, calibration burden)
- Single financial score + qualitative notes: rejected (not sortable/rankable)
