# ADR-006: Customer Mode isolation in Ask the Engine

**Status**: Locked  
**Date**: 2026-05 (v6.10.23)

## Decision

Ask the Engine has two modes — Internal and Customer — with strict isolation. Customer mode never sees internal data (vendor names, margins, scores, costs, tier classifications, pricing strategy) or any wiki page with `sensitive: true`.

## Context

AccentOS salespeople sometimes use a tablet in the showroom to answer customer questions. The same Knowledge Engine that powers internal analysis should not expose Accent's pricing margins or vendor relationships to customers.

## Internal mode

- System: "Accent Lighting Intelligence Engine" — expert in vendor comparisons, margins, specs, quoting
- Can reference vendor names, margin data, internal scores
- Wiki sources: all pages except `sensitive: true`
- Used by: Owner, Admin, Manager, Sales (backend)

## Customer mode

- System: "Accent Lighting Consultant" — warm, consumer-facing assistant
- NEVER reveals: being an AI on Claude, internal data, vendor names, margins
- When specific inventory unknown: route to showroom visit or personalized quote
- Wiki sources: concepts/ pages, lighting-reference cluster ONLY; skips entities/customers/, entities/vendors/ sensitive pages, all pages with `sensitive: true`
- Used by: showroom floor, future public embed

## Rationale

- Single chat component with mode toggle keeps codebase lean
- sessionStorage persistence: mode survives page navigation, resets on close (prevents accidental mode swap)
- Customer mode prompt explicitly forbids AI disclosure — legal/brand protection
- Wiki grounding in customer mode: safe to include lighting reference facts, not safe to include vendor deals or margins

## Consequences

- sendChat() checks chatMode before injecting wiki context
- `sensitive: true` frontmatter field in wiki pages enforces the boundary in code
- Future: customer mode could be embedded as an iframe on accentlightinginc.com (post M18 site approval)

## Alternatives considered

- Separate apps for internal vs customer: rejected (maintenance burden)
- No customer mode: rejected (showroom use case is real and recurring)
