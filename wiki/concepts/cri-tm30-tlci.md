# CRI, TM-30, and TLCI Standards

## CRI (Color Rendering Index) — Ra

Legacy metric. 0–100 scale; 100 = perfect color rendering under reference illuminant.

| CRI Range | Quality | Applications |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| 90–100 | Excellent | Retail, museum, medical, residential premium |
| 80–89 | Good | Office, commercial, most retail |
| 70–79 | Acceptable | Industrial, warehouse, utility |
| <70 | Poor | Storage, parking, outdoor areas only |

**Limits of CRI**: Ra averages 8 color samples, all pastel. Misses saturated colors (R9 = red saturation). A fixture can have CRI 90 but render reds poorly. Always ask for R9 separately.

**R9 threshold**: R9 ≥ 50 = good red rendering. R9 ≥ 80 = premium. Critical for: retail, food, medical, cosmetic applications.

## TM-30-18/20 (IES Technical Memorandum)

Two metrics: **Rf** (Fidelity) and **Rg** (Gamut).

| Metric | Range | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Rf | 0–100 | Average color fidelity (like CRI Ra, but 99 samples) |
| Rg | 60–140 | Color saturation. 100 = neutral; >100 = boosted saturation; <100 = muted |

**Rf ≥ 78** = code minimum for most commercial.
**Rf ≥ 85, Rg 95–110** = retail sweet spot (accurate + slightly vivid).
**Rf ≥ 90** = premium / museum / medical.

Rg > 100 makes colors pop (common in retail/food — "looks more vibrant"). But at Rg 120+ colors become unnatural.

## TLCI (Television Lighting Consistency Index)

Metric for broadcast/film. 0–100.
- TLCI ≥ 85: broadcast-acceptable
- TLCI ≥ 95: studio-grade

Relevant for Accent only when specifying showroom or studio lighting for content-creator customers.

## Practical spec checklist

When quoting commercial jobs, request from vendor:
1. CRI Ra (expect ≥80; specify ≥90 for retail)
2. R9 value (specify ≥50 for any food/apparel/cosmetic application)
3. TM-30 Rf and Rg (if customer is design-focused or upscale retail)
4. Correlated Color Temperature (CCT) tolerance: ±50K or ±100K?
