# Emergency Lighting Compliance

## Key codes

| Code | Scope | Requirement |
|------|-------|-------------|
| NEC Article 700 | Emergency systems | Automatic transfer in ≤10 seconds |
| NEC Article 701 | Legally required standby | Within 60 seconds |
| NEC Article 702 | Optional standby | As specified |
| NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code) | Egress illumination | 1 FC at floor, path of egress |
| IBC 1008 | Exit/egress lighting | ≥1 FC average, ≤0.1 FC minimum |
| IECC / ASHRAE 90.1 | Commercial energy | Emergency circuits exempt from power density |

## Egress illumination requirements (NFPA 101 / IBC)

- **Minimum at floor**: 1.0 FC (10.76 lux) along means of egress
- **Duration**: 90 minutes minimum on battery backup
- **Coverage**: every exit discharge, exit access corridor, stairway, ramp
- **Failure**: single lamp failure cannot reduce illumination below 0.2 FC

## Exit signs

- Letter height ≥ 6 inches
- Stroke width: ≥ 3/4 inch
- Visibility: legible from 100 feet (IBC)
- NEC 700.16: must be connected to emergency circuit
- Combined exit/emergency combos common in commercial — saves circuit wiring

## Battery backup types

| Type | Pros | Cons | Applications |
|------|------|------|-------------|
| Self-contained (integral) | Simple, no central battery | Periodic testing required | Small commercial |
| Central battery system | Centralized monitoring | Higher install cost | Large buildings |
| Generator transfer | Extended backup | 10-sec transfer delay | Critical facilities |
| LED emergency driver | Drop-in for LED fixtures | 90-min battery limit | Retrofit projects |

## Common products at Accent

- Emergency/exit combos (bug-eye + exit sign)
- LED emergency drivers (retrofit kits for existing LED fixtures)
- Self-contained emergency LED fixtures
- Remote-capable exit signs

## Testing requirements

- Monthly: 30-second functional test
- Annual: 90-minute full-duration test
- Documentation: test log required by AHJ
