# Rubric: Credit Terms (Weight: 6)

Days to pay after invoice. Longer terms = better cash flow = less working capital needed. Critical for managing inventory float.

## 0–10 Scale

| Score | Terms |
|-------|-------|
| 10 | Net 120+ |
| 9 | Net 90 |
| 8 | Net 75 |
| 7 | Net 60 |
| 6 | Net 45 |
| 5 | Net 30 (industry average) |
| 4 | Net 15 |
| 3 | COD with occasional flex |
| 2 | COD only |
| 1 | Strict prepayment required |
| 0 | No credit extended |

## Notes

- Net 30 is baseline industry standard for established distributors. Score 5.
- New vendor relationships typically start at Net 30 or COD; terms improve with payment history.
- Some vendors offer early-pay discount (e.g., 2/10 Net 30). Score on the base terms; note the early-pay option separately.
- Consistent late-payment history can cause term revocation — track in vendor notes.
