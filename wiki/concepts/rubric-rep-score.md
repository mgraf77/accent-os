# Rubric: Rep Score (Weight: 8)

The rep score rubric measures quality of the manufacturer's sales rep assigned to Accent. Covers responsiveness, proactivity, problem-solving, and relationship depth. One of 14 vendor scoring categories.

## 0–10 Scale

| Score | Rep quality |
|-------|------------|
| 10 | Proactive, full support — anticipates needs, solves before asked |
| 9 | Excellent — fast issue resolution, strong partnership |
| 8 | Very good — reliable, follows through, occasional delays |
| 7 | Good — reliable but rarely proactive |
| 6 | Adequate — handles requests, no above-and-beyond |
| 5 | Average — standard performance |
| 4 | Below average — slow, inconsistent |
| 3 | Reactive only — never initiates contact |
| 2 | Rarely helpful — hard to reach, poor follow-through |
| 1 | Unreliable — misses commitments, damages relationship |
| 0 | No rep assigned — Accent services account directly |

## Notes

- Rep quality is the most subjective category. Michael + sales team score collaboratively.
- Rep relationships are personal — individual rep departures can drop a vendor 3–4 points overnight.
- High-scoring reps: bring promo intel early, expedite claims, walk in unannounced to check displays.
- Reps covering too many accounts often score 3–5 regardless of intent.
- Track rep interaction history in AccentOS Rep Outreach module.
