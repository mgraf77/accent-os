# Rubric: IMAP / Markup (Weight: 8)

Internet Minimum Advertised Price (IMAP) protection and achievable retail markup multiplier. Higher IMAP = protected margin, reduced price-matching pressure from online competitors.

## 0–10 Scale

| Score | Markup multiplier |
|-------|------------------|
| 10 | 2.5× or higher (keystone-plus) |
| 9 | 2.3–2.49× |
| 8 | 2.1–2.29× |
| 7 | 2.0–2.09× (solid keystone) |
| 6 | 1.9–1.99× |
| 5 | 1.8–1.89× (average) |
| 4 | 1.6–1.79× |
| 3 | 1.4–1.59× |
| 2 | 1.2–1.39× |
| 1 | <1.2× markup |
| 0 | No IMAP / open pricing (race to bottom) |

## Notes

- Markup multiplier = MSRP ÷ net cost. A $100 net fixture with $200 MSRP = 2.0×.
- Vendors without IMAP enforcement create channel conflict — Amazon/online undercutters kill showroom margins.
- Strong DTC score ([[rubric-dtc]]) and strong IMAP often co-occur: vendors that protect dealers protect pricing.
- Lighting fixtures typically run 1.8–2.2× at showroom. 2.5×+ is premium territory (Hudson Valley, Visual Comfort).
