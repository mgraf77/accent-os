# SOP: Vendor Onboarding

Standard process for adding a new vendor to AccentOS and establishing the business relationship.

## Steps

### 1. Initial qualification (Michael + Sales)
- Confirm vendor is not a DTC-first brand (check [[rubric-dtc]])
- Verify no direct Amazon shop competing with dealers
- Check L1 membership status ([[rubric-l1-member]])
- Confirm they sell to independent lighting showrooms (not big-box exclusive)

### 2. Rep contact and intro meeting
- Follow [[sop-rep-outreach]] to establish rep contact
- Request: wholesale price list, net terms offer, freight policy, rebate program
- Key questions to ask vendor rep: IMAP enforcement policy, return/RGA process, co-op/MDF % available, rebate tier thresholds, freight minimums

### 3. Score in AccentOS
- Open Vendor Ranking → Add vendor
- Enter all 14 rubric scores based on rep conversation
- Mark categories as `unverified` until confirmed in writing
- Set data state = `needs_review` until first PO ships

### 4. Account setup
- Request credit application form
- Submit Accent's resale certificate / tax exemption
- Agree on terms in writing (net days, freight threshold, rebate tiers)
- Get login credentials for vendor portal (price lists, digital assets)

### 5. First order
- Place initial stocking order (qualify for freight threshold)
- Verify pricing against price list before PO submission
- Log PO in AccentOS → Purchase Orders

### 6. Showroom display
- If vendor has display program ([[rubric-display]]): request display pricing, current promo
- Plan floor placement — coordinate with Paul (showroom manager)
- Document display fixtures in AccentOS Showroom Displays module

### 7. Post-90-day review
- Update vendor score from `unverified` to `scored` based on live experience
- Adjust rep score ([[rubric-rep-score]]) from early interactions
- Verify all terms match what was promised in step 2

## Owner/contact

Michael Graf (Owner) approves all new vendor adds. Paul Graf handles floor placement.
