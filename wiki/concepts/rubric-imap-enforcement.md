# Rubric · IMAP Enforcement

**Weight:** HIGH. **What it measures:** vendor's willingness AND demonstrated action enforcing minimum advertised price (IMAP).

## Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **10** | Active enforcement, takes verifiable action against violators (warning letters, account holds, channel terminations) |
| **8** | Explicit enforcement policy, occasionally applied |
| **6** | Published policy but rarely enforced; no Accent-noted action in last 12 months |
| **4** | Informal enforcement only when escalated |
| **2** | Policy exists but openly ignored |
| **0** | No IMAP policy, OR vendor sells below MAP themselves |

## Why this is HIGH-weight

MAP violations directly erode Accent's margin and showroom relevance. The 2026-Q1 Minka Group response work documented 4 SKUs forced into manual GMC adds after channel partners undercut MAP. See [[sources/master]] §8 for documented evidence and [[map-violation-response]] for the escalation playbook.

## Compounds with

- [[rubric-dtc-consumer-direct]] — vendors who sell direct often have weak IMAP because they ARE the violator.

## Open loops

- Track every documented MAP violation against vendor IMAP score over time. If a vendor scores 10 here but has more than zero documented violations against Accent in trailing 12mo, downgrade to 6 and surface in Daily Brief.
