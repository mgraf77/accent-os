# Color Temperature Selection

## CCT scale reference

| CCT | Name | Appearance | Applications |
|-----|------|-----------|-------------|
| 2200K | Ultra-warm | Candlelight | Upscale restaurant, hospitality |
| 2700K | Warm white | Incandescent-like | Residential, restaurant, hotel |
| 3000K | Warm white | Slightly whiter | Retail, residential, hospitality |
| 3500K | Neutral white | Clean white | Office, retail, healthcare |
| 4000K | Cool white | Bright neutral | Office, educational, commercial |
| 5000K | Daylight | Blue-white | Warehouse, industrial, garage |
| 5700–6500K | Bright daylight | Cool blue | Task, security, outdoor |

## Selection by application

**Residential showroom recommendations**
- Living room: 2700K or 3000K
- Kitchen ambient: 3000K; task: 3000–3500K
- Master bath: 3000K (critical: cosmetic accuracy)
- Bedroom: 2700–3000K
- Outdoor: 3000K (warm) or 4000K (security)

**Commercial**
- Retail apparel: 3000K (warm, flattering)
- Retail food: 3000K (warm enhances appetite)
- Retail jewelry: 4000–4500K (cool, high sparkle)
- Office open plan: 3500–4000K
- Healthcare: 3500–4000K (exam rooms may need 4000–5000K)
- Warehouse/industrial: 5000K (alertness, high contrast)

## CCT consistency rule

All fixtures in a space should be within 100K of each other. Mixing 3000K and 4000K in the same room creates visible discontinuity. When a customer asks about mixing, recommend single-CCT throughout with accent layers at same CCT.

## Tunable white (TW)

Tunable white fixtures shift CCT from ~2700K to 6500K via dual LED channels. Used in:
- Healthcare (circadian support)
- Education (alertness tuning across school day)
- Hospitality (ambiance shifts from dining to morning briefing)
- Adds 20–40% cost premium over fixed-CCT

## Kelvin vs CRI (distinct metrics)

CCT is *color appearance* (warm/cool axis). CRI is *color rendering accuracy*. A 3000K, CRI 70 fixture looks warm but renders colors poorly. A 3000K, CRI 95 fixture looks warm AND renders colors accurately. See [[cri-tm30-tlci]] for quality metrics.
