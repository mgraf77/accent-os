# Rubric: DTC (Weight: 7)

How aggressively the vendor competes with its own dealers through direct-to-consumer sales. Lower DTC aggression = better for Accent's margins and competitive position.

## 0–10 Scale

| Score | DTC behavior |
|-------|-------------|
| 10 | No DTC — refers consumers specifically to Accent |
| 9 | No DTC — routes to lighting showrooms generally |
| 8 | Brand site exists, no checkout, dealer-only purchase path |
| 7 | — |
| 6 | — |
| 5 | Sells via showrooms + ecom dealers (Lumens/YLighting), no own ecom (average) |
| 4 | Showrooms + ecom dealers + light DTC |
| 3 | Sells through designers/trade only (exclusive channel) |
| 2 | Active DTC + Amazon — competing directly with dealers |
| 1 | Aggressive DTC — outranks dealers in Google search |
| 0 | Sells DTC on own website, no dealer protection |

## Notes

- Scores 7 and 6 are unused — the gap between "dealer locator only" and "ecom dealers" is real.
- Score 3 (designers/trade only) is unusual: premium brands like Visual Comfort trade-only means no consumer price competition but also limits Accent's consumer sales.
- Amazon presence is a major negative signal — it commoditizes the product and kills showroom justification.
- Strong IMAP ([[rubric-imap]]) combined with DTC score 5+ = well-managed channel.
