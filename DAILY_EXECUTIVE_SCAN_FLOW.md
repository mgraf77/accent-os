# DAILY EXECUTIVE SCAN FLOW — The 30-Second Triage

## 1. THE CONTEXT
The executive (Michael, Paul, or Patrick) opens AccentOS on their iPhone. They are likely mobile, between tasks, or starting their day. They do not want to "explore." They want to "resolve."

## 2. THE FLOW (SCAN → REVIEW → TRIAGE → CONFIRM)

### STEP 1: SCAN (0–5 Seconds)
*   **Visual Target:** The "Executive Strip" (The 6 Dots).
*   **Cognitive Goal:** Immediate situational awareness.
*   **Action:** If all dots are green/neutral, the scan is complete. If a dot is pulsing red or orange, move to Step 2.

### STEP 2: REVIEW (5–15 Seconds)
*   **Visual Target:** The Signal Feed (Vertical Feed).
*   **Cognitive Goal:** Understand the *why* behind the high-severity dots.
*   **Action:** Scroll top-to-bottom. The feed is sorted by **Severity First**.
    *   *Example:* "Lead Time Creep: Visual Comfort (V082) up 14 days."
    *   *Example:* "Stagnant Quote: $12k (Project X) silent for 14 days."

### STEP 3: TRIAGE (15–25 Seconds)
*   **Visual Target:** The Action Card (One per signal).
*   **Cognitive Goal:** Decide on the intervention.
*   **Options:**
    1.  **Confirm:** "Yes, I am aware and taking action" (Moves signal to "Tracked").
    2.  **Dismiss:** "Not a real issue" (Suppresses signal for N days, helps train the noise filter).
    3.  **Draft:** Tap to generate the "Resolution Asset" (e.g., pre-drafted email to the rep).

### STEP 4: CONFIRM (25–30 Seconds)
*   **Visual Target:** The Cleared Surface.
*   **Cognitive Goal:** Psychological closure. "I am on top of the business."
*   **Feedback:** A subtle "Operational Momentum" indicator showing that friction was reduced today.

## 3. FLOW CONSTRAINTS
*   **No Dead Ends:** Every signal must lead to an action or a suppression.
*   **No Scroll Fatigue:** If more than 5 signals are present, the noise filter is failing. The system must aggregate into "Situational Summaries."
*   **Offline Tolerance:** The scan must work on poor cellular data. Local storage mirrors the last known pulse.
